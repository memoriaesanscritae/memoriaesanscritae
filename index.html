A Public Archive of Sanskrit Thought and Vedic Practice

Memoriae Sanscritae is a long-term public reference archive dedicated to Sanskritic philosophy, Vedic ritual documentation, traditional texts, source notes, and living lineages of practice.

The site begins with philosophy — Advaita, Viśiṣṭādvaita, Dvaita, Śaiva Siddhānta, Mīmāṃsā, Nyāya, Sāṅkhya, Yoga and related traditions — before moving into ritual documentation such as Sandhyā Vandana, Upākarma, Samidhādāna, Tarpaṇa and other procedural traditions.

Structure

  • Philosophia — darśana, doctrine, schools, teachers, terminology.
  • Ritus — ritual procedures and traditional practice notes.
  • Textus — Sanskrit texts, mantras, ślokas, editions and translations.
  • Fontes — sources, scans, books, references and citations.
